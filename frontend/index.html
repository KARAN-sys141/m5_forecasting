The Engineering Architecture

This system tackles the Walmart M5 Forecasting Challenge, focusing strictly on the highly volatile FOODS category across 10 locations in 3 states.


1. The Zero-Inflated Problem

Over 61.7% of the raw data consisted of exact zeros (days where items didn't sell). Traditional Machine Learning models using RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error) fail here because they try to "guess" the exact day an intermittent spike happens, leading to severe over-forecasting and inventory wastage.


2. The Tweedie Masterstroke

Instead of forcing the model to guess spikes, the LightGBM architecture was configured with a Tweedie Objective Function (Variance Power 1.1). Tweedie acts as a mathematical bridge between the Poisson distribution (for counts) and the Gamma distribution (for continuous right-skewed data). It predicts the Statistically Expected Value rather than the exact daily count, perfectly mirroring how supply-chain safety stocks are maintained.


3. Feature Engineering at Scale

To avoid massive RAM crashes (OOM Errors), standard One-Hot Encoding was abandoned. Instead, an Extreme Target Encoding strategy mapped the Mean and Std. Dev of each specific Store-Item combination into two continuous features. Furthermore, "Release Date Trimming" was applied to remove 6 Million fake zeros from before items were actually launched.
